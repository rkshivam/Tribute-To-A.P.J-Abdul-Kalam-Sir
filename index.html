A . P . J . ABDUL KALAM

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in the small town of Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, emerged from modest beginnings to become one of India’s most revered scientists, visionaries, and leaders. Raised in a humble household, young Kalam’s curiosity, discipline, and love for learning set the foundation for a lifetime of groundbreaking contributions. After graduating in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology, he joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a pivotal role in developing India’s first indigenous satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in 1980. He became known as the "Missile Man of India" for spearheading the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme. His contributions extended far beyond science. In 1998, he played a crucial role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests, cementing India’s place as a nuclear power. Yet, Dr. Kalam remained deeply committed to using technology for national development, especially in rural and education sectors. In 2002, he became the 11th President of India, winning hearts as the "People’s President" with his humility, wisdom, and ability to connect with citizens of all ages—especially the youth. He used his platform to ignite minds, championing education, innovation, and national service as the pillars of a strong, self-reliant India. Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam remained a tireless educator, author, and mentor, traveling across the country to speak to students and encourage them to dream big and work hard. His books, including Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020, continue to inspire generations. On July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, he collapsed and passed away doing what he loved most—interacting with young minds. His death marked the end of an era, but his legacy continues to shine like a guiding light. This website is a humble tribute to Dr. Kalam’s extraordinary life—a life devoted to science, service, and the soul of India. His journey from Rameswaram to Rashtrapati Bhavan is not just a story of personal triumph, but a beacon of hope for every Indian who dares to dream.
